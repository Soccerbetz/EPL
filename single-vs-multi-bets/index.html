Single VS Multi-bets

Tristan Wiggill January 15, 2023 accumulators

Personally, I prefer to bet singles as they are the easiest bets to make. In 2-way betting markets (e.g Over/Under) these bets either win or they lose. Simple. Why complicate matters?

As already stated, consistently winning at sports betting is difficult. Most people lose money doing it. And, at it's core, profitable sports betting is about probabilities, mathematics and outsmarting the bookies. It is also about responsible bankroll management, understanding risk versus reward and skills development. 

It is improbable to expect that 10 or more bets will all land on a consistent enough basis to grow your profits. With every leg you add to an accumulator/multi/parlay bet, you decrease your probability of winning. 

To illustrate the point: say you have a five-leg multi-bet and each leg has implied decimal odds of 1.10. Using an online probability calculator, we see that the probability of all these legs landing is 62.1%. Should one leg fail, you lose the entire bet. Now ask yourself: How often does this happen to you? Is it really bad luck?

Sure, with multi-leg bets you multiply your potential payout. But, contrary to popular opinion, not by nearly enough since bookies love these type of bets and they load the commission/margin into them. More often than not, these multi-type of bets represent negative expected value which is multiplied by the number of picks.

Multi-bets are fun and exciting and they have a certain appeal, especially for casual bettors with a small bankroll. But the chances of getting 10 or more legs correct are extremely low, even at short odds. 

I for one get frustrated when a bet with short odds (say 1.10) loses! I find it far easier to accept losing a single bet with odds over 2.0. It is a fallacy to think that such short odds cannot lose. They do, primarily because they usually represent negative expected value. 

The good news though, is that it is possible to find positive expected value, even with odds as short as 1.06, for example. But you have to find it.
