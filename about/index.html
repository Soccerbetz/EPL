Crystal Palace VS Manchester United

Tristan Wiggill January 15, 2023

Crystal Palace come into this rescheduled midweek game on the back of a 1-0 loss away to fellow Londoners Chelsea.

Manchester United meanwhile came from a goal down to beat Manchester City 2-1 at Old Trafford on the weekend.

The Eagles have lost four of their last five Premier League games, scoring three and conceding 10. In contrast, the Red Devils have won all of their last five Premier League games, scoring 12 and conceding twice.

Palace has a negative goal difference, both home (-5) and away (-4). Troubling signs for Patrick Viera's men.

Goal Drought

Palace are finding it really difficult to score goals this season. While they have netted 17 times, just 11 of those goals have come from open play. As usual, Wilfried Zaha is the club's top goal scorer.

At 0.9, Palace are averaging less than a goal per game. They rank 19th in the league table when it comes to expected goals (xG). Man United ranks 3rd in the league when it comes to keeping clean sheets (8).

On this evidence, a win for Manchester United without reply seems logical.
